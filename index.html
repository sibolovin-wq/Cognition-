Mini-ACE Test for NZ Older Drivers

What is this test?

The Mini-ACE (Mini-Addenbrooke's Cognitive Examination) is a brief cognitive screening tool that takes about 5 minutes. It helps assess memory, attention, and thinking skills.

Why is it important?

In New Zealand, drivers aged 80+ are required to complete a cognitive assessment as part of their driver license renewal. This tool helps you practice what to expect.

What will you test?

  • Attention: Repeating number sequences
  • Memory: Remembering words immediately and later
  • Verbal Fluency: Generating words that start with a letter
  • Visuospatial: Drawing a clock and copying shapes
  • Language: Repeating sentences and naming objects
⚠️ Important: This is a practice tool only. The actual assessment should be conducted by a qualified healthcare professional. Don't worry about perfection—this is just to help you get familiar with the types of questions!

Scoring

The test is scored out of 30 points:

  • 26-30: Normal cognitive function
  • 23-25: Mild cognitive impairment (borderline)
  • Below 23: May indicate cognitive concerns
